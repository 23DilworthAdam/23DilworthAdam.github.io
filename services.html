Braille

SOMEBRAILLETHING

People who have a disability in one way or another have always been at a disadvantage compared to the majority. However, a French man named Valentin Hauy lessened the gap for some. He opened the Institut National des Jeunes Aveugles which in English is the Institute for Blind Youth. It was a school that made it’s own way to use books. Hauy made a raised letter alphabet, so the blind could read the textbooks.

45 years later, a student at one of his schools, designed a new system for the blind to read. This man’s name was Louis Braille. He based his system off of a coding method, that was intended for the army, called sonography. It was meant to be used for messaging without lights, but Braille had a different use for it. He made a system that had blocks made of 6 dots. His system was better than Hauy’s as it didn’t require as much space. Overall Braille’s system had 63 characters, and it could be read rapidly with fingertips.

Braille’s creation has made many people’s lives better by giving them something they needed. Countless people have gotten a better education. Books that they would’ve never been able to read were read. All of these lives were changed for the better due to Valentin Hauy making a school for the blind many years ago.
